About

Our Approach

The articles produced within the framework of Civilization or Barbarism are guided by a clear intention: to conduct investigations where attention tends to drift away, to explore off-the-radar topics, and to question areas left in blind spots.

We adopt a research-based approach. This means accepting trial and error, acknowledging mistakes, and embracing detours and backtracking. Here, thought is not a linear path, but a living movement.

This project offers neither final certainties nor pre-packaged discourse. If you are looking for the comfortable posture of a student receiving closed answers, you may indeed be disappointed.

However, if you accept uncertainty as a condition of thought, then these texts are meant to be read slowly, discussed, and revisited.

Why PDF format

The choice of PDF is deliberate. In a world designed for speed and constant scrolling, certain forms of writing require slowness.

PDF encourages a different relationship to text: careful reading, annotation, preservation, and discussion. These texts are intended to last, to be analyzed, shared, and taught.

Our Method

  • Rigorous exploration of off-the-radar topics
  • Acceptance of mistakes and reversals
  • Production of high-quality texts and documents, updated as new insights emerge
  • Openness to discussion and educational sharing

Sensitive Topics

Some texts published within Civilization or Barbarism may address sensitive or controversial subjects. Our intention is neither to offend nor to provoke gratuitously.

However, any research-based approach sometimes involves examining hypotheses that fall outside conventional frameworks of discussion. Certain avenues remain unexplored not due to a lack of intellectual interest, but because of conformity, social caution, or simple diplomatic restraint.

It also happens that attention focuses on a detail or phrasing rather than the underlying question — a well-known phenomenon summarized by the old saying: “when the finger points at the moon, some look at the finger.”

We believe that honest reflection must be able to examine these areas with rigor and calm, even when they create discomfort. To explore does not mean to assert definitively: it means to accept examining what deserves to be examined.

Our Commitment

We do not provide ready-made certainties. If you are looking for comfortable answers, this site is not intended for that.

However, if you accept uncertainty as a driving force of thought, you will find here texts to read slowly, to question, and to pass on.
